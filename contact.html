FAQ's

Are we at the mercy of the wind?

We are able to control the ‘up and down’ of the balloon and we can rotate like a globe. The wind does play a large factor in where the balloon flys. This is why we do intense wind checks so that we can plan our route and take off and land in a safe area.


What time do we meet?

Meet times vary depending on the day of your flight. The best ballooning weather is early morning, when it’s cooler and winds are calm. We meet an hour and half before take off. We need this time to sign waivers, do the safety briefing and inflate the balloon. Typically, plan to be at the meeting area at 6:30am.


Is it going to be cold?

Dress warm. You can always take layers off. There is also a fairly large flame above your head that might keep you a tad Warmer.


Are landings hard?

Landing are soft, tiptoe and comfortable when there is no wind, which is why we meet so early in the morning. In the event that the wind does pick up we’ll do a standard high wind landing, during which the basket will drag a bit before stoping and it might tilt. We’ll brief you how to handle a high wind landing and the basket tilting over. It is not dangerous, as long as you follow the safety instructions, and you’ll probably just end up have a big laugh. High wind landings do not happen often.


How many people will the basket fit?

Squeezed in like sardines we can fit 7 including the pilot. Depending on the temperature and how much the balloon will lift, 4 or 5 passengers is best.


We have a larger group than 7, is that possible?

Yes. You can split your group up and we can fly separate flights. The group members not in the balloon will be in the chase vehicle which follows the balloon wherever it may land. An adventure on its own!
